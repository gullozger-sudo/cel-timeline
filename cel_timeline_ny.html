Touring strategy — Gull's proposal

Proposed by Gull Øzger (GullBakken) — for discussion at next meeting.

★ Proposal from Gull — June 2026

Tour Phase 3 and Phase 4 works immediately after premiere — while the cast is assembled

The most cost-effective moment to tour a production is directly after its premiere, while the cast is already together, the work is fresh, and technical rider, staging, and logistics are already prepared. Touring planned in advance — built into the production budget from the start — is significantly cheaper than returning to tour later.

I propose that we plan a touring circuit for both the Phase 3 shared work and the Phase 4 independent performances as an integral part of the EEA project — not as an afterthought. Specifically:

  • After the Phase 3 premiere in Warsaw (September 2028): immediately tour the shared work to 2–3 Norwegian venues before the cast disperses, using existing EEA travel and accommodation allocations.
  • After the Phase 4 premieres (2029): tour the independent performances to partner venues in both countries and Serbia — again while cast and production are assembled.
  • Build touring fees, accommodation, and travel for each venue into the EEA production budget from the start. This is standard in Scandinavian performing arts applications and is viewed positively by EEA assessors as evidence of dissemination reach.
  • Galleri Nordistica in Belgrade (Alexandra Kremenets) offers a ready-made venue for an exhibition and/or showing in Serbia — no additional partner-building needed.
  • The dance film can be screened independently at venues where a full performance is not possible — expanding reach with minimal extra cost.
